Parking: Description back to ToC

Figure 1 shows the conceptual model of the ontology with its classes and relationships (object properties).

A parking lot is a place where vehicles can be temporarily parked by paying the fee that has charges. Parking lots are a (sub)class of Equipment which, in turn, can have observation points. This class and property are described in the Ontology for the representation of a municipality's infrastructure. There are two (sub)classes of parking: those that are on-street parking and those that are off-street parking. On-street parking can be part of a regulated parking zone, which is a (sub)class of regulated zone. All parking areas have parking spaces that can be grouped according to the type of permit; for example, there are groups of spaces for residents, people with reduced mobility, etc. They are also grouped by the type of vehicle, for example, spaces for cars, for motorcycles, etc. These two taxonomies have been defined in the Ontology for the representation of the census or register of vehicles.

It is located on a section of Serrano Street in the Salamanca district of Madrid. The rate applies during specific hours, it is part of the SER Zone "001", and it is managed by the Madrid City Council. It has a total of 25 spaces for residents.
